New Brunswick HST: 15%

Need to calculate reverse HST from a New Brunswick total? Use the TaxBackwards.ca reverse HST calculator.

New Brunswick charges 15% Harmonized Sales Tax (HST) on most taxable goods and services. HST combines the 5% federal GST and the 10% provincial component into a single tax, fully administered by the Canada Revenue Agency. For every $115.00 paid in New Brunswick, $100.00 is the net consideration base and $15.00 is HST. Businesses registered for HST with the CRA can recover the full $15.00 as an Input Tax Credit.

How New Brunswick HST Reverse Calculation Works

To extract the net pre-tax amount from a gross New Brunswick total, divide by 1.15. To isolate the HST portion, subtract the net from the gross — or equivalently, multiply the net by 0.15.

Worked example 1: $115.00 gross ÷ 1.15 = $100.00 net. $115.00 − $100.00 = $15.00 HST. The $15.00 is ITC-eligible for a CRA-registered business.

Worked example 2: $230.00 gross ÷ 1.15 = $200.00 net. $230.00 − $200.00 = $30.00 HST.

HST Exemptions in New Brunswick

The following categories are zero-rated or exempt from HST under federal CRA rules that apply uniformly across all HST provinces, including New Brunswick:

  • Basic groceries (most food intended for human consumption)
  • Prescription drugs and biologics
  • Most medical devices and assistive technologies
  • Residential rent and related services
  • Most health and dental services
  • Most financial services and insurance premiums

New Brunswick and HST: Background

New Brunswick was one of the three original Atlantic provinces — alongside Nova Scotia and Newfoundland and Labrador — to adopt the Harmonized Sales Tax in April 1997, when HST replaced both the federal GST and the provincial sales tax. This long-standing unified structure means NB businesses have operated under a single-rate, single-authority tax system for nearly three decades, simplifying filing compared to GST+PST provinces.

New Brunswick has no general point-of-sale rebate for the provincial component of HST. The standard 15% rate applies to all taxable supplies unless the supply falls under a zero-rated or exempt category defined in federal legislation. Businesses with cross-border activity between NB and Quebec should note that Quebec uses a separate QST system rather than HST.
