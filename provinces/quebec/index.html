Quebec GST + QST: 14.975%

Need to separate GST and QST from a gross Quebec total? Use the TaxBackwards.ca reverse sales tax calculator.

Quebec applies two separate taxes to most purchases — the 5% federal Goods and Services Tax (GST) administered by the Canada Revenue Agency, and the 9.975% Quebec Sales Tax (QST) administered by Revenu Québec. Both taxes apply directly to the same net consideration base, for a combined effective rate of 14.975%. For every $114.975 paid, $100.00 is the net amount, $5.00 is GST, and $9.975 is QST.

How Quebec GST + QST Reverse Calculation Works

To extract the net pre-tax amount, divide the gross total by 1.14975. To isolate each tax, multiply the net by its rate: 0.05 for GST and 0.09975 for QST. Neither tax compounds on top of the other — both apply independently to the same net base.

Worked example 1: $114.98 gross ÷ 1.14975 = $100.00 net. GST: $100.00 × 0.05 = $5.00. QST: $100.00 × 0.09975 = $9.98. Total tax: $14.98.

Worked example 2: $229.95 gross ÷ 1.14975 = $200.00 net. GST: $200.00 × 0.05 = $10.00. QST: $200.00 × 0.09975 = $19.95. Total tax: $29.95.

Note: since January 1, 2013, QST is no longer calculated on top of GST — it applies directly to the net consideration base at 9.975%.

GST Input Tax Credits and QST Input Tax Refunds

Businesses registered for GST with the CRA can claim the GST portion as an Input Tax Credit (ITC) to reduce their net federal tax remittance. Businesses registered for QST with Revenu Québec can claim the QST portion as an Input Tax Refund (ITR). The GST and QST components must be tracked and reported separately — they are remitted to different tax authorities.

Quebec and GST + QST: Background

Quebec is the only province in Canada that did not adopt HST. When the federal Goods and Services Tax was introduced in 1991, most provinces eventually harmonized their provincial tax with the GST into a single HST return. Quebec chose a different path, retaining its own Quebec Sales Tax (QST) administered by Revenu Québec, separate from the Canada Revenue Agency. As a result, businesses operating in Quebec must register with two tax authorities — the CRA for the 5% GST, and Revenu Québec for the 9.975% QST — and file two separate consumption tax returns.

An important structural change took effect on January 1, 2013: QST is now calculated directly on the net consideration base at 9.975%, not on top of the GST-inclusive amount. Before 2013, there was a minor compounding effect because QST applied to the GST-included price. Under the current non-compounding structure, the combined effective rate is exactly 14.975%. GST recovery uses the Input Tax Credit (ITC) mechanism filed with the CRA; QST recovery uses the Input Tax Refund (ITR) mechanism filed with Revenu Québec — two distinct recovery systems that must be tracked separately.
