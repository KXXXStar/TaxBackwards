Nova Scotia HST: 14%

Need to calculate a reverse HST extraction? Use the TaxBackwards.ca calculator.

Nova Scotia charges a 14% Harmonized Sales Tax (HST) on most taxable supplies, effective April 1, 2025 (reduced from 15%). HST is a single combined federal and provincial tax, fully administered by the Canada Revenue Agency — there is no separate provincial tax authority for HST in Nova Scotia. For every $114.00 paid in Nova Scotia, $100.00 is the net consideration base and $14.00 is HST. Businesses registered for HST with the CRA can claim the full $14.00 as an Input Tax Credit.

How Nova Scotia HST Reverse Calculation Works

To extract the net pre-tax amount from a gross Nova Scotia total, divide by 1.14. To isolate the HST, subtract the net from the gross — or equivalently, multiply the net by 0.14.

Worked example 1: $114.00 gross ÷ 1.14 = $100.00 net. $114.00 − $100.00 = $14.00 HST. The $14.00 is ITC-eligible for a CRA-registered business.

Worked example 2: $228.00 gross ÷ 1.14 = $200.00 net. $228.00 − $200.00 = $28.00 HST.

What Is an Input Tax Credit (ITC)?

If your business is registered for HST with the CRA, the HST you paid on eligible business expenses is recoverable as an Input Tax Credit. The ITC reduces your net HST remittance to the CRA. Use the extracted HST amount from this calculator as your ITC-eligible figure, and verify eligibility against CRA guidelines before filing.

Nova Scotia HST Exemptions

Basic groceries, prescription drugs, most financial services, and insurance premiums are zero-rated or exempt from HST in Nova Scotia. Nova Scotia also provides a point-of-sale rebate on the provincial component of HST for children's clothing and footwear — meaning qualifying children's items effectively bear only the federal 5% GST portion at time of purchase. For a complete list of zero-rated and exempt supplies, see the CRA guide on taxable, zero-rated, and exempt supplies.
