Nova Scotia HST: 14%

Need to extract HST from a gross Nova Scotia total? Use the TaxBackwards.ca reverse HST calculator.

Nova Scotia charges a 14% Harmonized Sales Tax (HST) on most taxable supplies, effective April 1, 2025 (reduced from 15%). HST is a single combined federal and provincial tax, fully administered by the Canada Revenue Agency — there is no separate provincial tax authority for HST in Nova Scotia. For every $114.00 paid in Nova Scotia, $100.00 is the net consideration base and $14.00 is HST. Businesses registered for HST with the CRA can claim the full $14.00 as an Input Tax Credit.

How Nova Scotia HST Reverse Calculation Works

To extract the net pre-tax amount from a gross Nova Scotia total, divide by 1.14. To isolate the HST, subtract the net from the gross — or equivalently, multiply the net by 0.14.

Worked example 1: $114.00 gross ÷ 1.14 = $100.00 net. $114.00 − $100.00 = $14.00 HST. The $14.00 is ITC-eligible for a CRA-registered business.

Worked example 2: $228.00 gross ÷ 1.14 = $200.00 net. $228.00 − $200.00 = $28.00 HST.

What Is an Input Tax Credit (ITC)?

If your business is registered for HST with the CRA, the HST you paid on eligible business expenses is recoverable as an Input Tax Credit. The ITC reduces your net HST remittance to the CRA. Use the extracted HST amount from this calculator as your ITC-eligible figure, and verify eligibility against CRA guidelines before filing.

Nova Scotia HST Exemptions

  • Basic groceries (most food intended for human consumption)
  • Prescription drugs and biologics
  • Most medical devices and assistive technologies
  • Most financial services and insurance premiums
  • Children's clothing and footwear — Nova Scotia provides a point-of-sale rebate on the provincial component of HST, meaning qualifying items effectively bear only the federal 5% GST portion at time of purchase

Nova Scotia and HST: Background

Nova Scotia was one of the three founding HST provinces, adopting the Harmonized Sales Tax in April 1997 alongside New Brunswick and Newfoundland and Labrador. The HST rate at adoption was 15% — a 5% federal component combined with a 10% provincial component — and that rate held unchanged for nearly three decades, spanning multiple provincial governments of different political stripes. In October 2024, Premier Tim Houston announced a reduction of the provincial component from 10% to 9%, describing it as the largest tax break in Nova Scotia history. The new 14% combined rate took effect on April 1, 2025, marking the first HST rate change in Canada since Prince Edward Island adjusted its rate in 2016.

All Nova Scotia HST is administered by the Canada Revenue Agency on a single combined return — there is no separate provincial tax authority for HST in Nova Scotia. The full 14% is ITC-eligible for registered businesses; there is no split between the federal and provincial components for recovery purposes. Nova Scotia also maintains a point-of-sale rebate on the provincial component of HST for children's clothing and footwear, which reduces the effective tax on those items to the 5% federal portion. Outside of that specific rebate, there are no general Nova Scotia-specific point-of-sale rebates on the provincial component for the public.
