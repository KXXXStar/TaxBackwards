How to Remove HST from a Price in Nova Scotia

Nova Scotia charges 14% Harmonized Sales Tax (HST) on most goods and services. When you're given a tax-included total and need to work backwards to the pre-tax price — for expense reports, business records, or just curiosity — you need the reverse HST formula.

The Reverse HST Formula (Nova Scotia 14%)

The formula is straightforward:

Pre-tax amount = Total ÷ 1.14

Nova Scotia HST breaks down as 5% federal GST plus a 9% provincial component, for a combined rate of 14%. To isolate the HST from a gross total:

  • HST rate: 14% (5% federal GST + 9% provincial component)
  • Formula: Pre-tax = Total ÷ 1.14
  • HST amount: Total − Pre-tax

4 Worked Examples

Total Paid Pre-Tax Amount HST (14%)
$11.40 $10.00 $1.40
$56.99 $49.99 $7.00
$113.00 $99.12 $13.88
$282.50 $247.81 $34.69

Example 1 — $11.40 total: $11.40 ÷ 1.14 = $10.00 pre-tax. $11.40 − $10.00 = $1.40 HST.

Example 2 — $56.99 total: $56.99 ÷ 1.14 = $49.99 pre-tax. $56.99 − $49.99 = $7.00 HST.

Example 3 — $113.00 total: $113.00 ÷ 1.14 = $99.12 pre-tax. $113.00 − $99.12 = $13.88 HST.

Example 4 — $282.50 total: $282.50 ÷ 1.14 = $247.81 pre-tax. $282.50 − $247.81 = $34.69 HST.

What If You're a Business? (Input Tax Credits)

If your business is registered for GST/HST, you may be able to claim Input Tax Credits (ITCs) on eligible business expenses. For Nova Scotia purchases, only the federal 5% GST portion is recoverable as an ITC — the provincial 9% component is not. ITC eligibility depends on your registration status and the nature of the expense; consult a tax professional for advice specific to your situation.

Nova Scotia HST Exemptions

Not every Nova Scotia purchase includes 14% HST. Common exempt and zero-rated items include:

  • Basic groceries
  • Prescription drugs
  • Most medical devices
  • Child car seats and booster seats

If an item is exempt, there is no HST to remove — do not apply the ÷ 1.14 formula to those totals.
